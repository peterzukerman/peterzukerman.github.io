About

I'm a masters student in University of Washington's CLMS (Computational Linguistics) program. I'm also working as a natural language processing researcher at Infoscitex.

Most of my work involves helping machines understand human language. I work on a variety of projects, including machine translation, question answering, and information extraction. I have also worked on projects involving natural language generation, sentiment analysis, and text summarization. I have an interest in low resource languages, having worked with Siberian Yupik, a language spoken by less than 1000 people.

Currently, I'm working on automatic transcription systems for specific domains of English.
